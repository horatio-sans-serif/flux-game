# Flux — Training & Development

Flux training is built on a single practice structure that stays constant from a
beginner's first day to elite play. What changes is depth: the same arc is
revisited and deepened over years. Phase length and weekly schedule are decided
together by student and teacher.

---

## 1. The Universal Practice Arc

Every practice session, at every level, moves through five sections in order:

1. **Ballet**
2. **Rhythm** — nightclub and rhythm ballroom
3. **Martial arts**
4. **Field** — drills, light cardio, practice plays, scrimmages
5. **Smooth ballroom** — Waltz, Viennese Waltz, Argentine Tango

The arc is constant from beginner to elite; the content deepens over time.

---

## 2. Training Levels

| Level                        | Stage                                     |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| **Universal — Beginner**     | Early elementary school                   |
| **Universal — Intermediate** | Late elementary school                    |
| **Program**                  | Middle school; splits into a chosen track |

At the **program level**, students choose one of three tracks:

- **Striking** — deepens into boxing and karate.
- **Grappling** — deepens into judo and BJJ.
- **Clinch / Hybrid** — deepens into Muay Thai and wrestling.

Each track carries complementary dance deepening alongside it.

**Always available, at every level:**

- Group classes for ballet, fundamentals refreshers, and mixed scrimmages.
- Accelerated programs and camp intensives for older or adult beginners.
- Informal rec games, playable anywhere, with no institutional structure needed.

---

## 3. Assessment & Advancement

To advance a level, a student is assessed on:

- **Practical skills**
- **Sparring** — scored on _points_, not on winning.
- **Theory**

**Assessment rules:**

- A panel of the regular instructor and an outside assessor evaluates the student.
- **Both must agree** to advance the student.
- Before reassessment, a student needs a minimum number of classes and clearance
  from their instructor.

## 4. Martial Arts Phases

### Beginner

| Phase | Name                            | Focus                                                                              |
| ----- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1** | Solo Movement                   | Safe falling, katas, padded block-balance drills. Practicable at home.             |
| **2** | Partner Observation & Mirroring | Cycling partners, verbal observation, copying and returning to one's own movement. |
| **3** | Contact & Basic Grappling       | Basic holds, introductory sparring, building on Phases 1–2.                        |

### Intermediate

| Phase  | Name                         | Focus                                                                               |
| ------ | ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **4**  | Refinement                   | Sharpening beginner material; more complex katas; technique becomes automatic.      |
| **5**  | Integration                  | Striking and grappling flow together; transitional partner drills.                  |
| **6**  | Opponent Reading & Reaction  | Reading telegraphs, real-time response, shrinking reaction time.                    |
| **7**  | Flux-Specific Conditioning   | Stamina tied to game scenarios: sprint-to-spar, circle-defense duration.            |
| **8**  | Strategic & Mental Layer     | When to engage, when to avoid, decision-making under pressure.                      |
| **9**  | Vital Zones & Scoring Theory | Full scoring map, anatomy as instinct, point-accumulation drills.                   |
| **10** | Live Flux Sparring           | Full game-condition sparring; individual style emerges; post and beat hints appear. |

## 5. Dance Phases

### Beginner

| Phase | Name                           | Focus                                                                |
| ----- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1** | Execution & Understanding      | Correct, precise steps; basic concepts; follows direction easily.    |
| **2** | Group Floor Work & Observation | Group mirror work, dancing around others, basic routines introduced. |
| **3** | Beyond Baby Ballet             | First contact with advanced technique; preview of intermediate.      |

### Intermediate

| Phase  | Name                          | Focus                                                                               |
| ------ | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **4**  | Sharpening                    | Technique gets precise; higher standards; constant corrections.                     |
| **5**  | Integration & Flow            | Flowing between ballroom styles; fluid ballet combinations.                         |
| **6**  | Black Swan / White Swan       | Improvisation and mirroring; copying and countering a partner instinctively.        |
| **7**  | Physical Limits               | Pushing the body's boundaries; staying sharp under fatigue; rapid-fire drills.      |
| **8**  | Intelligence & Restraint      | When to push, when to add flair, when to stay quiet.                                |
| **9**  | Instant Reaction              | Reading the music and the partner's vibe; responding without thinking.              |
| **10** | Full Integration & Assessment | Ballroom competitions and marathons; full ballet routines and solos under pressure. |

---

## 6. Dance at the Program Level

At the program level, dance continues as ballet and ballroom, plus whatever
additional styles a student feels help their own performance.
