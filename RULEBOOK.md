# Flux — Official Rulebook

Flux is an outdoor team sport that blends ball-handling, territory control, and
one-on-one sparring. Two teams race to pocket balls into elevated stands while
scoring points through controlled combat. The balls are the clock; the spar
points are the score.

---

## 1. Overview

- **Players:** Two teams (called _cells_) of 4–5 players each.
- **Setting:** Outdoors. All you need is a field, the circles, and stands.
- **Field:** A standard soccer field, scaled to the age group.
- **Officials:** One referee, using a standard refereeing system.
- **Equipment per player:** A team-colored uniform. No protective equipment is worn.

The game ends when one cell pockets its third ball, but the winner is the cell
with the most **spar points**, regardless of who closed out the game.

---

## 2. The Field

- **Circles:** Six circles are placed on the field, each with a _stand_ at its center.
- **Circle diameter:** 20–30 feet.
- **Layout:** There is no fixed configuration. Circle placement varies game to
  game, subject to a minimum distance between circles. The layout is revealed to
  both cells before the match begins.
- **Stands:** Each stand is 15 feet tall. At the top is a 2-foot pocket — four
  branches forming a square, with a net strung between them.
- **Center field:** Where balls start and where pocketed or knocked-out balls are
  returned to play.
- **Professional fields:** Feature stands (spectator seating) running entirely
  around the field.

---

## 3. The Ball

- Shaped like a soccer ball.
- Textured like a football (grippy surface for carrying and gripping).

---

## 4. Uniforms

- **Only rule:** Players must wear their team's color.
- **Starter pack (all in team color):** short-sleeve shirt, tank top, long-sleeve
  shirt, shorts, pants, looser shorts, socks, headbands.
- Everything beyond the starter pack is personal expression.
- Teams and clothing are unisex.
- **No protective equipment.**

---

## 5. Objective & Scoring

- **Closing out the game:** The first cell to pocket all three of its balls into
  stands ends the game.
- **Winning:** The cell with the highest spar points wins — regardless of which
  cell closed out the game.
- **The principle:** _Balls are the clock. Spar points are the score._

---

## 6. Ball Rules

- **Start:** Two balls sit at center field. A new ball is introduced each time one
  is pocketed.
- **Possession:** A ball may only be _held_ inside a circle. No kicking, rolling,
  or passing it.
- **Sparred carrier:** If a ball carrier is sparred in the open field, the ball
  drops and freezes in place.
- **Knocked-back balls:** Any ball knocked back into play is immediately thrown to
  center field by the referee.
- **Out of bounds:** If a ball touches the ground outside the field boundary, play
  is _out_ and the ball returns to center.

---

## 7. Movement & Play

Flux has its own vocabulary for how players move and where play happens.

**Actions:**

- **Run** — moving freely with no ball.
- **Carry** — moving with the ball.
- **Relay** — passing the ball between teammates.
- **Drive** — a coordinated push toward a stand with teammates.

**Zones of play:**

- **Groundwork** — open-field play.
- **Fieldwork** — play inside the circles.
- **Circuit** — how players transition between groundwork and fieldwork.

---

## 8. Circle Rules

- **Occupancy:** First come, first served.
- **Challenging:** Any player may challenge an occupied circle.
- **Defense:** The defending cell does not get to choose who defends — the
  challenger's positioning decides the matchup.
- **Losing a defense:** If a cell loses a circle defense, the ball is knocked out
  (KO'd) to center field.

---

## 9. Sparring

Sparring is Flux's combat element. It happens **inside circles**, or in the open
field when **explicitly initiated**.

**Scoring by hit location:**

- **Vitals (high value)** — a vital hit _closes out_ the spar (ends it immediately).
- **Joints (low value).**
- **Inconsequential areas** — no value.

**Point rules:**

- The loser's accumulated spar points are subtracted from their cell's total.
- The points from a vital hit do not transfer to the winner — they simply end the spar.

**Interception:**

- A teammate may intercept (step into a spar) if they are not serving a penalty
  and they call the interception in time.

**Conduct:**

- Vital hits run on the **honor system**.
- **Closing in** — surrendering a spar — is always a valid choice.

---

## 10. Melee

- **Called when:** all currently sparring players call "melee" simultaneously.
- **Effect:** everyone not in the melee goes to the sidelines.
- Melee fighters still count as field presence.
- **Minimum:** at least one player must remain on the field at all times.

---

## 11. Penalties

- **Called by:** the referee or a player. A player's call does not stop the clock.
- **For:** deliberate, malicious hits.
- **Duration:** 2 minutes.
- **Restriction:** a player serving a penalty cannot intercept for teammates.
- **Basis:** modeled on a composite MMA rulebook.

---

## 12. Vital Zones

A **vital zone** is any point on the body that would kill within three minutes or
less if struck with a weapon. Vital hits are governed by the honor system.

---

## 13. Game Start

1. Both cells line up on opposing sides.
2. Two balls are placed at center field.
3. The referee's whistle starts the **dash** — the opening sprint.

---

## 14. Game End

A game can end in any of the following ways:

- **Close out** — a cell pockets its third ball.
- **Time rule** — after two hours, either cell has the mandatory right to call the
  game.
- **Surrender** — from exhaustion or injury.
- **Mutual deferral** — both cells agree to defer between games.

---

## 15. Substitutions

- Up to the coach and players.

---

## 16. Post-Game Culture

- Cells greet each other before the game.
- Players bow after each spar.
- Handshakes after the game.
- Both cells stretch together in a circle afterward.
- Younger players say something good about each person on both cells.

---

## 17. Organization

| Level      | Meaning                                      |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Cell**   | 4–5 players — the basic unit (a team).       |
| **Circle** | A regional collective of cells.              |
| **Court**  | The national / international governing body. |

Competitive play begins when kids are ready. Intense play is not introduced
before ages 10–11.

---

## 18. Posts (Player Roles)

Each cell is built around five posts, organized into two pairs and a bridge.

| Post      | Role                                                        |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Kova**  | The _caller_ — absorbs information and makes decisions.     |
| **Kela**  | The _strategist_ — observes and reads the field.            |
| **Kora**  | The _connector_ — bridges the strategic and physical pairs. |
| **Koda**  | The _anchor_ — physical and instinctive.                    |
| **Kreva** | The _chaos agent_ — exploits blind spots alongside Koda.    |

**Pairings:**

- **Strategic pair:** Kova + Kela
- **Physical pair:** Koda + Kreva
- **Bridge:** Kora connects both pairs

---

## 19. Beats (Player Assignments)

A _beat_ is a functional assignment layered on top of a post. Posts and beats are
assigned independently by the coach, so **every combination is unique**.

| Beat     | Role        |
| -------- | ----------- |
| **-van** | Runner      |
| **-bin** | Carrier     |
| **-lid** | Defender    |
| **-jab** | Striker     |
| **-sub** | Interceptor |

---

## 20. Coaching

A coach is responsible for:

- Understanding strategy and players deeply.
- Diagnosing where a player is going wrong and how to fix it.
- Assigning posts and beats.
- Clearing students for reassessment.

---

| Term                         | Meaning                                                    |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dash**                     | The opening sprint.                                        |
| **Pocketing**                | Successfully getting a ball into a stand.                  |
| **KO**                       | Knocking a ball back into play from a lost circle defense. |
| **Intercept / Interception** | Stepping in for a teammate in a spar.                      |
| **Melee**                    | Called when all sparring players call it simultaneously.   |
| **Closing out a spar**       | Winning via a vital hit.                                   |
| **Closing in a spar**        | Surrendering a spar.                                       |
| **Time rule**                | The two-hour mandatory game-end call.                      |
| **Challenging**              | Contesting an occupied circle.                             |
| **Closing out a game**       | When a cell pockets its third ball.                        |
| **Penalty call**             | A foul call by the referee or a player.                    |
