# Flux — Quick Start (Pickup Game)

The one-page version. Read the [RULEBOOK](RULEBOOK.md) for the full rules.

## What you need

- An open field (any size — scale it to the players).
- 6 **circles** marked on the ground (cones, chalk, or rope), each with a tall
  **stand** in the middle (a pole with a net pocket at the top).
- 2 grippy balls to start.
- **Velcro flag setup:** every player wears ribbons that pull away (flag-football
  style). The ribbons at **vital spots** (chest/back, throat-line) end a spar when
  pulled; ribbons at **joints** (shoulders, hips, knees) score small points.
- Two teams (**cells**) of 4–5, one in **red**, one in **blue**.
- One referee.

## The 30-second explanation

> Carry balls into the stands to pocket them. Pull ribbons off opponents to score.
> **Balls are the clock. Ribbon points are the score.**
>
> The game _ends_ when one team pockets its 3rd ball — but the team with the most
> ribbon points _wins_, even if the other team ended the game.

## How a game runs

1. **Line up** on opposite sides. Two balls sit at center. Greet the other cell.
2. **Whistle = the dash.** Everyone sprints in.
3. **Get a ball, carry it to a stand, pocket it.** You can only safely hold a ball
   inside a circle; if someone spars you in the open field, you drop it.
4. **Spar to score.** Sparring happens inside circles (challenge whoever holds it)
   or when someone calls it in the open. Pull a **joint ribbon** = small points.
   Pull a **vital ribbon** = the spar ends instantly.
5. **Watch your points.** When you lose a spar by a vital pull, the points you've
   built up get subtracted from your team — so a high scorer is a juicy target.
6. **Closing out:** the first cell to pocket 3 balls ends the game. But if you're
   _behind on points_, ending the game makes you lose — so sometimes you stall at
   2 balls and hunt for points first.

## The one big idea

You are always playing two games at once: the **ball race** (the clock) and the
**ribbon duel** (the score). Winning means timing the end of the ball race for a
moment when you're ahead on ribbons.

## Etiquette

Bow after each spar. Handshakes and a group stretch after the game. With younger
kids, everyone says one good thing about each player on both teams.
